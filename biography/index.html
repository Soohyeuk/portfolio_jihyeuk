BIOGRAPHY

New York City-raised Korean-American cellist Jihyeuk Choi has been rapidly building his international reputation as a soloist and a chamber musician since his solo debut in 2017 with the City Chamber Orchestra of Hong Kong. ​Having the honor of winning international competitions such as the 6th New York International Competition, the 4th Hong Kong International Competition, and the Chamber Music Society of Lincoln Center Youth Chamber Music Competition, Choi has appeared in professional venues throughout Asia and the United States including the Weill Hall/Zankel Hall at the Carnegie, Alice Tully Hall at the Lincoln Center, DiMenna Center, Merkin Hall, Recanati Hall, Bar-Uryan Hall, Jordan Hall, M. Friedberg Hall, Hylton Performing Arts Center, Hong Kong City Hall, and the IBK Chamber Hall at the Seoul Arts Center.

Choi has supplemented his professional studies by participating in international music festivals including the Keshet Eilon International String Mastercourse, the Clarion Society Collegium, the Heifetz International Festival, and the Bowdoin International Music Festival. Choi also has appeared in masterclasses with Sir Steven Isserlis, Alban Gerhardt, Steven Doane, Paul Katz, Natasha Brofsky, Hillel Zori, Jeffrey Ziegler, Myron Lutzke, and Támas Varga.

A proud alumnus of the Peabody Institute of the Johns Hopkins University, Choi was conferred the Bachelor of Music degree as the recipient of the full scholarship under the tutelage of Amit Peled along with mentors including Leon Fleisher, Seth Knopp, Benjamin Pasternack, Maria Lambros, Marian Hahn, and Michael Kannen. Choi is currently pursuing the Master of Music degree at the New England Conservatory as a recipient of the Donald and Alice Noble Scholarship, under the tutelage of Lluís Claret, along with mentors including Laurence Lesser, Kim Kashkashian, and Merry Peckham. Choi is also a member of the Trio Nomad, New England Conservatory’s Honors Ensemble for the 2023-2024 season. Jihyeuk Choi performs on an unnamed cello made in ca. 1827 from Markneukirchen, Germany.
